Orbit, A Fancy Planner

A circular 24-hour planner for visual time blocking, recurring routines, templates, and drag-based daily planning.

Launch Web App Private GitHub Repo

Core concept

Orbit visualizes a full day as a circular 24-hour dial: midnight at the top, noon at the bottom, and the entire day visible at once instead of compressed into a vertical list.

The interface uses two concentric rings so larger blocks and smaller events can occupy overlapping time without visually colliding.

Notable features

Circular day viewPlan around a full 24-hour dial instead of a linear calendar.
Two planning layersSeparate larger time blocks from granular events.
Drag editingMove blocks, resize edges, and shift items between rings.
TemplatesSave reusable daily structures and apply them quickly.
Recurring tasksSupport repeat logic and regenerate tasks after completion.
Month viewZoom out from daily planning into broader scheduling.

Interaction model

  • Tap an empty arc to create a block.
  • Tap a block to edit its details.
  • Drag a block around the dial to move it.
  • Drag edge handles to resize the block duration.
  • Drag between rings to change planning layer.
  • Lock items to protect important blocks from accidental clearing.

Design direction

Orbit uses a dark editorial aesthetic with serif and monospace typography, muted tones, and clock-like spatial structure. The goal is to make scheduling feel less like spreadsheet maintenance and more like arranging the shape of a day.
